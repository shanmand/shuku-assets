Executive Summary

Period: July 2025 - January 2026

Strong Top-Line Growth Amidst Operational Shifts

The Quick Service Restaurant (QSR) sector continued its strong upward trajectory during the seven-month period, driving overall net revenue growth of 9.6% year-on-year, meeting budget expectations.

  • Durban (KZN): Interventions to manage ingredient costs are proving effective. Casual diners underperformed.
  • Johannesburg: Increased electricity costs due to extended equipment runtimes. Offset successfully by direct cost savings.
  • Overheads: Unplanned maintenance across both regions caused overspending in support services.
  • EBITDA: Closed R11.2 million ahead of budget, up 57.5% YoY, reflecting solid operational resilience.

Operational Updates & Risk Mitigation

Infrastructure and Asset Recoveries

Asset Recovery: Pallets & Crates

  • Lost Pallets: CHEP audit confirmed 2,487 lost pallets. Settlement reached inclusive of a 10% discount and a negotiated R74.4k rental adjustment. Matter is now closed.
  • Crate Audit: Concluded claim with Vector (accepted 19k transferred crates). Settlement invoice of R535k paid in Jan 2026.
  • CHEP finalizing end-of-January crate count to determine final loss. Adjustments to future payments expected by end of February.

Utilities: Power Outages

  • Honeydew Substation: 183 hours of power outages recorded YTD due to municipal grid faults.
  • Diesel & Paraffin Impact: Increased shifts triggered higher-than-budgeted paraffin usage (77,898 liters vs 74,334 liters budget).
  • However, net paraffin *costs* saw a favorable usage variance despite the price per liter jumping to R18.42 (vs R18.00 budget).
Transport Note: Sales and distribution costs rose primarily due to heightened Hungry Lion volumes, replacement costs for lost pallets, and required overtime/additional drivers in the Johannesburg region.

Conclusion & Forward Outlook

H2 Strategy & Easter Planning

Positioned for Exceptional Full-Year Growth

Despite a volume softening across all sectors in January, year-to-date sales targets were met and costs managed effectively across most departments.

  • Growth Catalysts: The QSR segment remains the primary driver. Double-digit gains from Chicken Licken, Nando’s, Pedros, and Hungry Lion continue to offset sluggish supermarket channels.
  • Remediation Focus: In KZN, aggressive efforts will remain on restoring margins impacted by high ingredient costs. In JHB, tight monitoring of maintenance and transport overruns is mandated.

Easter 2026 Outlook

As we approach the Easter period, we anticipate a sharp volume increase in February and March. Production planning for Hot Cross Buns is complete and expected to deliver strong seasonal spikes.


Based on the current trajectory, Lupo Bakery is positioned to achieve impressive, highly profitable EBITDA growth over the previous financial year.
